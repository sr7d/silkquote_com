What should a professional quotation include?

A professional quotation should include your company name and contact information, the client's name and billing address, a unique quote number and issue date, a valid-until date, an itemized list of products or services with quantities and unit prices, a subtotal, any applicable tax and discounts, a grand total, and your payment terms or notes. Clear, itemized quotes reduce disputes and build trust before any work begins.

Quotation vs estimate vs invoice vs proposal

A quotation is a fixed-price commitment; once accepted, the price is binding. An estimate is an approximate cost that may change as scope becomes clearer. An invoice is a payment request sent after work is completed or goods are delivered. A proposal is a broader document covering approach, methodology, and pricing for complex engagements, often leading to a formal quotation once scope is agreed. For most business transactions, a clear quotation followed by an invoice is all you need.
