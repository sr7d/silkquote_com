What is a quotation generator?

A quotation generator is an online tool that lets you create professional price quotes — also called sales quotations or price proposals — without any software to install. You enter your company and client details, add line items with quantities and prices, and the tool calculates totals automatically. The result is a clean, printable PDF quote you can send to customers in minutes.

How to create a quotation in 5 steps

  1. Add your company details Enter your company name, address, phone, and email in the top-left of the quote. This becomes your letterhead.
  2. Fill in the client's billing information Add the client or company name, contact person, and their address in the "Bill To" section.
  3. Add your line items Click "＋ Add Line Item" for each product or service. Enter a description, quantity, and unit price — the row total calculates automatically.
  4. Set tax, discount, and valid-until date Enter a tax rate (%) and any discount amount. Set a valid-until date so your customer knows when the price expires.
  5. Download as PDF Click "⬇ Download / Print PDF" and choose "Save as PDF" in your browser's print dialog. The navigation and toolbar are automatically hidden from the printout.

What is the difference between a quote and an invoice?

A quotation is a price offer sent before a sale — it tells the customer what something will cost and is typically valid for a set period. An invoice is sent after the work is done or goods are delivered, requesting payment for an agreed amount.

Can I add my company logo to the quote?

How do I download the quote as a PDF?

Click the "⬇ Download / Print PDF" button, then choose "Save as PDF" in your browser's print dialog (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari all support this). The site header, footer, and toolbar are hidden automatically so only the quote document prints.

What should a professional quotation include?

A professional quotation should include: your company name and contact information, the client's name and address, a unique quote number and date, a valid-until date, an itemized list of products or services with quantities and unit prices, a subtotal, any applicable tax and discounts, a grand total, and your payment terms or notes.
